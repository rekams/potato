10 Things You Never Knew About Potatoes

  1. The Incas measured time by how long it took to cook a Potato.
  2. Potatoes were first introduced to Europe in the sixteenth century but were not accepted becuase they had not been mentioned in the Bible.
  3. To popularize Potatoes in France Antoine Augustin Parmentier placed armed guards around his Potato field. They had instructions to accept all bribes and allow people to steal the Potatoes.
  4. The French Fry was first served at a Presidential dinner with Thomas Jefferson.
  5. The word Potato comes from the Sspanish Patata.
  6. Today there are over 5000 varieties of Potato.
  7. August 19th and October 27th are National Potato Days.
  8. In 1995 Potatoes were the first plants grown in space on the space shuttle Columbia.
  9. Potatoes have more vitamin C than an Orange, more potassium than a Banana and more fibre than an Apple.
  10. The largest Potato ever grown weighed 3.76kg.

Potato of the Month: The Vitelotte

  • Description: This is a French variety of blue-violet Potato. It has a nutty flavour and smells of Chestnuts.
  • Health Benefits: It has antioxidant fighting free radicals, helps improve heart health and is low in calories.
  • Interesting to know: The name Vitelotte comes from the Archaic French word Vit meaning penis because of the phallic shape of the potatoes.
