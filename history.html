The History of the Potato

Origins:

It is thought that potatoes have been around for a long time: the earliest archaeologically verified potato tuber is from the coastal site of Ancon in mordern day central Peru dating from 2500 BC. However, we think the Incans have been cultivating them from 8000 BC.

Introduction to Europe:

After the Spanish invaded South America they started to bring materials, such as poatoes, back to Europe. It is thought that the Potato entered Europe as early as 1536. It is not known exactly when Potatoes came to Ireland and there is some debate as to how they came to Ireland. Some say floated into Cork from the wreckage of the Armada while others claim it is from Sir Walter Raleigh planting them at his Cork estate in 1589.

The Potato Famine:

Between 1845 and 1849 a disease called Late Blight caused the Potato Crop to fail in Ireland. Leading to the death of about one million people from starvation and the emmigration of around two-and-a-half million people to America.

Today:

Today Potatoes are the worlds fourth largest food crop (after maize, wheat and rice). Over three thousand varities of cultivated potato can be found as well as over two hundred species of wild potato. They come in russet, red, white, yellow and purple.
